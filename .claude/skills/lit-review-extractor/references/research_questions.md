# Research Questions — Community/Equity Dimensions of Mobility & Transportation Infrastructure

Edit this file to add, remove, or reword research questions. `init_csv.py`
parses the `RQ<number>` labels below to generate CSV columns, so keep that
numbering format intact (e.g. "RQ1.", "RQ2.").

RQ1. How has access to mobility and transportation historically been distributed unevenly across communities (by race, income, and geography)?

RQ2. What planning and engineering mechanisms (highway routing, transit siting, redlining) have produced or reinforced this uneven distribution?

RQ3. What is the distinction between procedural and distributive equity in transportation planning, and how has the field historically privileged one over the other?

RQ4. What documented precedent exists for infrastructure being planned or deployed without community participation, and what were the consequences (exclusion, displacement, erosion of trust)?

RQ5. How does participatory or community-engaged planning change outcomes, legitimacy, or trust in transportation infrastructure projects?

RQ6. How is "mobility justice" framed as distinct from or broader than "transportation equity," and what dimensions does it add (e.g., embodiment, relationality, capability)?

RQ7. How do efficiency-driven values from transportation engineering become embedded in infrastructure design, and whose priorities do they represent?

RQ8. What does infrastructure studies literature say about infrastructure as a carrier of values, becoming visible only through breakdown or exclusion?

RQ9. Are there existing case studies of new mobility technologies (ride-hail, micromobility, AVs) arriving in communities and reproducing or disrupting existing equity patterns?

RQ10. Where does the literature stop short of framing mobility as an ongoing, relational practice — as opposed to a one-time distributive or access problem?
